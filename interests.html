Professional Stuff

I am entering my senior year in the electrical and computer engineering program at the University of Colorado, Boulder and will be focusing on dynamics and controls along with embedded systems. I figure that people will pretty much always need robots and stuff to control robots, so controls mixed with embedded systems is probably a pretty safe career choice. Of course, that wouldn't really mean anything if I didn't enjoy the material.

At the time of writing this (mid August, 2017) I have yet to take any controls classes, but am signed up for two in the coming school year. I am actually really excited for these because I think it's going to give me a chance to actually incorporate a lot of the things I have learned during my time at CU. It should also let me continue to dive deeper into the embedded systems world, which will be great.

I took my first embedded systems course as my sophomore elective, and it was amazing. I have always been interested in how smaller, specialized devices work, and now I actually have some experience with making those devices. That class really helped me to better understand programming and direct memory manipulation, but also helped to spark an interest in system control in me. After taking that class, I started working at the Colorado Space Grant Consortium at CU, working on the RocketSat-X 11 team from CU. I worked on the avionics team where we designed and created a deployment system for a composite slit tube boom.

I worked at Halleck Willard Inc. over the summer of 2017 as an embedded systems intern. While there I worked primarily on porting code and designing hardware to mimic the Eggbot Pro with a Renesas S1 microcontroller. This was a great experience for me; not only did I gain some experience with a new microcontroller, but I also learned how to more efficiently read and understand code written by another developer that wasn't necesarily intended to be understood easily. Working with the Renesas S1 chip and the Synergy Software Package also allowed me to learn some of the basics of working with an RTOS.
